# Companion Scientific Paper

This directory contains the primary scientific and engineering paper underlying this repository:

**Reaction–Transport Regime Analysis for Desulfurization of Gas and Petroleum Streams: An Engineering Diagnostic Framework**

**Author:** Ahmad Saylam  
**Year:** 2026  
**DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.20095695](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20095695)

## Scope

The paper develops a reaction–transport–separation framework for diagnosing desulfurization performance across natural gas, light hydrocarbons, diesel-range fuels, and heavy petroleum fractions.

The framework considers:

- hydrodesulfurization
- oxidative desulfurization
- catalytic oxidation and sweetening
- adsorptive and reactive adsorptive desulfurization
- radical-assisted oxidation
- external mass transfer
- internal diffusion
- adsorption and surface phenomena
- hydrodynamic intensification
- downstream separation
- energy-normalized sulfur removal
- reactor selection and scale-up validation

## Engineering Purpose

The central objective is to identify whether observed desulfurization performance is controlled by intrinsic reaction kinetics, external mass transfer, internal diffusion, adsorption, downstream separation, energy input, or a mixed regime.

The framework is intended to support technically defensible decisions in experimental design, process diagnosis, reactor selection, process intensification, and scale-up.
